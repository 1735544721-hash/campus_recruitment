Abstract

University employment services face three major pain points: fragmented job information, disconnected application workflows, and delayed feedback. Students often struggle to complete job searching, resume submission, and interview tracking on a single platform, while companies lack a unified entry for resume screening and communication. To address these issues, this study designs and implements a campus recruitment system based on Spring Boot. The frontend is built with Vue 3 and Element Plus, providing job search, company profile display, resume management, application records, interview scheduling, and message notifications. The backend uses Spring Boot 3.2 and MySQL, integrates MyBatis-Plus and JWT to unify business entities such as users, companies, positions, resumes, applications, interviews, and messages, and applies transaction control and index optimization to ensure data consistency and process traceability. The system supports role-based access for students, companies, and administrators, records application status transitions and interview outcomes, and offers an administrator portal for supervising users, companies, and positions. Functional testing covers login authentication, position publishing, resume submission, interview scheduling, and message notifications. The results indicate stable system operation and improved recruitment efficiency and information transparency.

Keywords: campus recruitment; Spring Boot; Vue 3; MySQL; resume management
